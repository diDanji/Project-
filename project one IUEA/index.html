WAY OF LIFE OF THE KUKU

people of kajokeji

kuku people

The Kuku are atribe of Karo people from Southsudan.They inhabit the agricultural lands of kajokeji county in central Equatorial state.The kuku speak a Bari dialect,also called kuku.Theys are chiefly a farming people

Home stead

The kuku are found in southeastern part of central Equatoria.Their most important town is kajokeji..

Economic activity

They are chiefly a farming people relying on mixed farming.During the rainly season they grow sudstantial food crops,mainly sorghum(also known in sudan as dura) maize,millet,cassava,sweet potatoes,and beans(loputu).in the dry season they manage a small scale of cattle,goats and sheep herding.

Way of life of the kuku

The kuku lifestyle is reflected in their religious belief and practices.They strongly believe that there is only one God and he lives somewhere above the skies.In practice, they believe that all happenings to a family,a clan or the whole tribe take place as a result of their deeds.God is merciful and kind,speaks and acts to the people through their ancestors.The word God in kuku is Njun

kuku tribe

The kuku are called karo people, speaking Bari dialect,madi/pojulu,kuku,and kakwa clans,luygbara family and individuals

Intermarriages

When it comes time for a young man to be married, a family will go to a neighbouring community and get information about a girl in another family that is old enough to get married.If her personality and reputation is acceptable, a dwory(usually a pre determined number of animals) is paid to the bride's family but now day the young man will be the one to look for the girl both will be in relationship before now involving the elders of the land.
